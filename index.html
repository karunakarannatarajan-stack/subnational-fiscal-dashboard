Macro-Fiscal Insight: The Deficit Quality Matrix

This quadrant chart compares all states on two critical fiscal metrics: Revenue Balance (X-axis) and Capital Outlay (Y-axis). The size of each bubble represents the state's total Gross Fiscal Deficit. Hover over bubbles to see details.

Top-Right (Golden Quadrant): States here run a Revenue Surplus and maintain high Capital Outlay. Their fiscal deficit is entirely used for productive asset creation, representing high fiscal prudence.

Bottom-Left (Vulnerable Zone): States here run a Revenue Deficit and have low Capital Outlay. They are borrowing money primarily to fund daily consumption (salaries, interest) rather than growth.

Cross-State Debt Sustainability Framework

These charts compare states across three critical sustainability dimensions: Debt Burden, Fiscal Flexibility, and Growth-Cost Spread.

The Spread Matrix: A state's debt is mathematically sustainable if its Nominal GSDP Growth Rate (g) exceeds Effective Borrowing Cost (r). States with a positive (green) spread can theoretically outgrow their debt over time, while a negative (red) spread risks a debt spiral.

Burden vs Flexibility Quadrant: States in the bottom-left have low Debt-to-GSDP and low Interest-to-Revenue ratios (high flexibility). States in the top-right are highly leveraged, where debt servicing eats up a huge chunk of tax revenues, crowding out development.

Cross-State Revenue Quality & Fiscal Autonomy

A state's fiscal autonomy is determined by its **Own Tax Revenue (SOTR) to GSDP ratio** vs. its **Federal Transfers Share (devolutions + grants-in-aid) to total revenue receipts**.

Bottom-Right (High Autonomy): States here generate high own tax revenues and rely less on the Federal government. This grants them significant policy flexibility to design state-specific welfare and capital programs.

Top-Left (High Dependency): States here have a high dependency on Central Transfers. They are highly exposed to macroeconomic shocks, changes in Finance Commission devolution formulae, and Central policy shifts.

Rigid Expenditures and Off-Budget Risks

Top-Left (Fiscal Space): States here have lower Committed Expenditure (salaries, pensions, interest) and higher Capital Outlay. They have the flexibility to adjust budgets during shocks and can invest heavily in infrastructure.

Bottom-Right (Trapped): High committed spending (>60% of revenue receipts) leaves very little room for productive capital outlay. Additionally, watch out for high Contingent Liabilities (off-budget guarantees) which can suddenly fall on the state's books.

Understanding the 3D Fiscal Space

Mapping states in 3D helps analyze relative performance on multiple axes simultaneously:

Fiscal Leverage Cluster: States grouped near the top (high Z-axis / Debt) and far-left (low X-axis / Own Tax). These states face tight constraints and require cautious borrowing to avoid structural stress.

Fiscal Autonomy Cluster: States grouped near the right (high X-axis / SOTR) and top-right (high Y-axis / Capex) with low Z-axis debt. These states have strong capacity to finance growth on their own.
